Maps

This map is a part of the mapping Sustainable Development Goals project, made in QGIS. It depicts the Sustainable Development Goal indicator 11.1.1: Proportion of urban population living in slums, informal settlements or inadequate housing. I was inspired by the images presented by the professor on the first day of the course, which showed the townships in Cape Town, South Africa. They revealed the problem of a lot of the informal housing not being mapped on the official maps and therefore not receiving attention when decisions about the location of services and utilities are being made. I wanted to see to what degree that is the problem on a more global scale.

For the map I chose to use different values of blue as my color scheme, with the darker shades of blue representing high values of the percentage of population living in inadequate housing. The color blue was choses, because I wanted to use a color that is fairly accessible to color blind people and doesn’t have very strong correlation with “danger” or other negative aspects that could negatively impact people living in slums by facilitating stereotypes.

When looking at the final map, it is important to note the amount of missing data. For example, China, which has a really large population and, while it does not have slums per se, it does have problems with adequate housing. Thus, due to the missing data, the map does not show the full scale of the problem.

This map is an interactive WebMap made with the use od ArcGIS Online and ArcGIS Apps. It presents the same SDG Indicator as the previous map, however, both values from 2010 (left) and 2018 (right) are presented. The map shows only some countries improving, such as Sudan, a bigger number increasing their percentage of people living in inadequate housing, and an increased number of countries not submitting their indicator values in 2018.

The design choice to put the data from those two years next to each other provides a straightforward way of monitoring the possible progress in the goal. The colors yellow and dark grey were used to provide a greater contrast between no data and the five feature categories that were created using equal interval classification.

This map shows is a final product of the Remote Sensing assignment in Google Earth Engine. It shows the change of snow cover in the mountainous region of Tatra Mountains from Winter 2021 to Fall 2022. Since snow cover is a major component of climate, a fresh water source and a flood source, it is important to study its changes. Additionally, tourism is one of the major employers and a lot of the tourism is connected with the snow, for example, through the operation of ski resorts.

The layers show the region in the respective season both in true color composite and in a false color composite that makes snow cover appear ark blue or black, vegetation is dark and light green and urban features white, grey, cyan and purple. Additionally, there is a Spring layer with the Normalized Difference Snow Index that renders the snow cover white and everything else dark grey. The images used for these layers come from Sentinel 2 satellite. Lastly, a true color composite of the entire year from the Landsat 9 satellite data is included.
