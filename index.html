Skils

The projects created during my GIS course required the use of a variety of tools and skills, which I am now proficient with. They included: use of GIS specific software, GI Science skills and programming skills.

GIS software

  • ArcGIS including ArcGIS Online and ArcGIS apps
  • QGIS
  • Google Earth Engine

GIS skills

  • Implementing the Map Making Workflow
  • Working with raster and vector layers
  • Working with Cartograms, Cholopleth maps and Dasymetric Mapping
  • Processing remote sensing data
  • Collecting and processing field work data
  • Creating webmaps and geoapps
  • Evaluating and implementing map design consideration (projections, colors, area units and basemaps; utilizing visual hierarchy and negative space)
  • Basics of special analysis (Site Suitability Analysis, Voronoi Polygons Tessellation, Network Analysis)

Programming skills

  • Creating a website
  • HTML basic proficiency
  • GIS specific use of Java
  • Proficient use of GitHub
  • Design considerations

About me

Hello!

My name is Agnieszka Kubica. Currently, I am a bachelor student of University Collage Utrecht, majoring in Economics and Mathematics with a minor in Statistics. In my further education and carrier, I plan to pressure Data Science.

An important thing about me is that I love being challenged. It is the trait that always shaped my life. It is the reason why I chose the International Bachelorette Programme in high school and followed it with additional subject on top of the curriculum. It was a major point in my decision to leave the familiar and comfortable home in Poland, and move to the Netherlands to peruse my bachelor degree in Liberal Arts and Sciences. It is why I constantly pick up new skills and throw myself into new projects. Finally, it is the reason why, last year, I took the Programming for Research course (UCACCMET2J), which changed my plans for the future. Programming and data visualization tuned out to be immensely interesting and using those skills in my own projects filled me with joy and satisfaction. After the course, I knew it was something I want to pressure further.

This website presents the outcome of another course I took to continue on that journey. One of the things that was underlined throughout this GIS course was that the maps we create tell a story that can be heard by decision makers and the general public. I think that ability to collect, analyze and visually present data has the potential to change the world and I would like to be a part of this process.
